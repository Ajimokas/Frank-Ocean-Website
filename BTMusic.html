Frank Ocean - Behind The Music

Solo - 2016

Article link

Solo by Frank Ocean was released in 2016, under the citically accalimed Blonde album. It has a key signature of 3 flats in the key of E major and Ocean mostly sings the song in falsetto. The song maily talks about the pain of livig life "solo" and the pain of growing up as you become more isolated as an adult. It is a double entendre that could mean "solo" as in alone or "so low" as in feeeling downtrodden or sad. One of the ways in the song Frank copes with the feeling of sadness is through marijuana intoxication, where he sings "inhale, in hell there's heaven". This signifies that in the chaotic, melancholic world he is in, the drug substance provides some sort of calm that keeps him in a "heavenly" state. Although a rather peculiar song, the song charted the US Billaboard Hot 100 at 98 and the RnB/HipHop Billaboard at 38.

The list of collaborators on the song include:

Jazmine Sullivan for vocals, organ played by James Blake, Vocals by Frank Ocean and Mars 1500 on the keys

Fun Fact:

Solo samples Todd Rundgren's "Flamingo"
